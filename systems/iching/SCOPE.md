# I Ching v0.3 scope

This slice supports a reproducible three-coin cast, six bottom-to-top line values, moving-line detection, primary and changed hexagrams, trigram decomposition, the conventional King Wen number and name, and a bounded structural report.

The classical layer registers two public-domain *I Ching* edition locators and a public-domain historical source for the Zhu Xi count-routing lineage. It exposes two explicit moving-line policies: report every moving line or route by changing-line count. It records edition metadata and the fact that the two *I Ching* editions have not yet been textually collated.

v0.3 attaches the Library of Congress and Project Gutenberg source IDs directly to primary and changed hexagram identity facts. Coin generation remains project-specified, so classical provenance is not incorrectly attached to randomness or casting-protocol claims.

It does not bundle classical translations or commentaries, claim that the count-routing policy is universal, add nuclear or mutual hexagrams, claim predictive validity, or substitute for high-impact professional advice.
